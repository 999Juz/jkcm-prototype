Understanding Chinese Medicine

A system built on thousands of years of listening.

Chinese Medicine doesn't treat symptoms — it treats you. Here's what that means and why it works well for women's hormonal health.

01

East meets West — a different way of seeing

Western medicine and Chinese medicine ask fundamentally different questions. Western medicine asks: what disease is this, and how do we suppress it? Chinese medicine asks: why is this person's energy out of balance, and how do we help them restore it?

"When a number of people with similar symptoms see a Chinese medicine practitioner, they will likely receive totally different treatments. This is Chinese medicine treating the person, not the disease."

— Dr Justin Keane

02

How Chinese Medicine works

The aim of Chinese Medicine is to restore and maintain the natural order of your energy. Pain and dysfunction are your body telling you where it is struggling.

Qi — your vital energy

When Qi stagnates — as it commonly does under stress or hormonal fluctuation — you feel irritable, tight, blocked.

Blood (Xue) — nourishment

Nourishes the body, anchors the mind and supports the menstrual cycle. Blood deficiency is one of the most common findings in women with fatigue and poor sleep.

Yin and Yang — fundamental balance

The hormonal changes of perimenopause and menopause often reflect a decline in Kidney Yin — the cooling, nourishing aspect of our constitutional energy.
